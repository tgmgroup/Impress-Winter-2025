Fall is a great time of year to get out of the house and into nature. In Gunma, there are lots of fun places to visit and fall activities to participate in*. Here are some recommendations* from Dai 2 Junior High School:

* participate in: 参加する
* recommendation: おすすめ

1 / 5

Have you been feeling a little cold as the weather changes? Check out* some of the many onsens at Kusatsu or Ikaho. Or if you like getting out in the forest head over to* Tsumagoi and spend a night camping.

* check out: 調べる
* head over to: ～に行く

2 / 5

The various mountains in Gunma are also beautiful this time of year due to the changing of the leaves. Mount Akagi or Mount Myogi are great places to hike. And if you’re feeling hungry, there is a great apple farm in Numata.

 

3 / 5

But if you just want to stay indoors*, head over to Konnyaku Park in Tomioka, where you can try making your own konnyaku jelly.

* stay indoors: 家にいる

4 / 5

There are so many great things to do in Gunma in the fall! What do you like to do in the fall?

If you like traveling, here are some interesting places for you to visit! These two places are the origins* of some of the scariest stories in Japan. Read on and learn about them!.

* origin: 由来（ゆらい）

1 / 6

1. Himeji Castle

Did you know that the movie Ring comes from the beautiful Himeji Castle? This movie is based on the story Banshu Sarayashiki.

 

2 / 6

The Story of Banshu Sarayashiki

The evil Tetsuzan says Okiku stole a treasured* plate*, then kills* her and throws her into a well*. If you go to Himeji Castle, you can see that well... and you might hear her counting plates from the bottom of the well.

* treasured: 秘蔵（ひぞう）
* plate: お皿
* kill: 殺す
* well: 井戸

3 / 6

2. Osorezan

This mountain’s name is Mount Fear*, so it must be a scary place. It is said that people can cross a river here, the Sanzu no Kawa, to enter Hell.

* fear: 恐怖（きょうふ）

4 / 6

There is also a temple here, Bodai-ji, that holds a festival to talk with the dead.
